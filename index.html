ABOUT ME

From Journalist to Novelist

I write about the tragicomedy of corporate life
from the tranquility of cottage country.

As a recovering business journalist, I base my stories on the absurdities and contradictions I've observed in the business world. Those experiences, combined with a deep curiosity about what makes us tick, inform every page I write.

My work explores the gap between who we think we are and who we actually become in the pursuit of success—and what happens when those two versions collide.

Before turning to fiction, I spent more years than I care to count as a business journalist, editing and writing for national publications and witnessing firsthand the human drama that unfolds in boardrooms and cubicles alike. The characters in my novels are composites of real people I've met, observed, and sometimes been.

Compelling fiction does more than entertain—it illuminates. It helps us see ourselves and our world with fresh eyes. Whether I'm writing about corporate culture, personal ambition, or the quiet desperation of modern life, my goal is always the same: to tell stories that resonate long after the final page.

When I'm not writing, you'll find me in cottage country, where the distance from the city provides the perfect perspective for reflecting on the absurdities I once lived daily. I'm currently working on a sequel to Losing It.
